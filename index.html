Open Genome Sequencer

A decentralized effort to democratize whole genome sequencing

Motivation

Genomics is rapidly becoming the central pillar of medicine. Diagnosis, prevention, drug discovery, and personalized therapies are increasingly dependent on sequencing.

Today, sequencing remains concentrated within institutional and commercial infrastructures. If genomic technology remains closed, access, innovation and sovereignty over biological data risk becoming constrained.

This project explores the possibility of an open, reproducible, community-driven genome sequencer.

The goal is not disruption for its own sake, but resilience: ensuring that knowledge and tools for reading DNA remain accessible, inspectable, and improvable by independent researchers and advanced biohackers.

Project Scope

  • Exploration of nanopore and alternative sequencing paradigms
  • Open hardware signal acquisition and electronics
  • Flow cell experimentation
  • Open basecalling and signal processing pipelines
  • Community reagent experimentation and validation
  • Documentation-first research

Roadmap

Phase 1 — Foundations

  • Literature review and architecture definition
  • Open electronics prototyping
  • Signal simulation datasets

Phase 2 — Experimental Platform

  • Prototype flow cell experiments
  • Low-noise acquisition system
  • Initial basecalling models

Phase 3 — Iterative Research

  • Nanopore optimization experiments
  • Error correction pipelines
  • Library preparation experimentation

Phase 4 — Community Sequencer

  • Reproducible hardware design
  • Open protocols
  • Benchmarking and validation

Who We Seek

This project is intended for advanced contributors:

  • Biohackers with wet-lab experience
  • Signal processing engineers
  • Microfluidics specialists
  • Protein engineers
  • Embedded hardware engineers
  • ML researchers working on basecalling
  • Open science advocates

Principles
